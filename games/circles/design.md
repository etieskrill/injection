fleeting:
- vis receptacles collect vis from the environment
- specific types of receptacles may accept great directly transferred amounts (e.g. corporeal vis, or whatever i'm gonna call handheld magic)
- stream receptacles constantly draw a limited amount from their source, but require an initial bit of vis to get going
- storage receptacles may store some amount of vis, but collect none of their own
- battery receptacles may store a greater amount of vis, but only collect and release it slowly
- capacitor receptacles may store a lesser amount of vis, but collect and release it very quickly
- circles are stream receptacles with lesser efficiency as a simplification
- receptacles can filter for vis type, or completely block all with aux runes
- aux runes affect filtering on the circle they sit on, as well as the next clockwise primary rune slot (how doe for different vis types)
- stored vis degrades/evaporates, depending on the amount of vis present in a component, and more slowly for batteries, and more quickly for capacitors
- primary runes take in vis in blocks of some minimal amount, often of some polarity, and use it to achieve a certain effect
- transformer runes transform or combine an amount of vis into another amount of vis of any polarity
- emitter runes consume vis to produce an output
